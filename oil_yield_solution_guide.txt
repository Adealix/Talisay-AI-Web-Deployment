================================================================================
   TALISAY AI — OIL YIELD CALCULATION: SIMPLIFIED SOLUTION GUIDE
   Single Analysis Scan — Green, Yellow, and Brown Fruits
================================================================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 VARIABLES (Inputs to the Formula)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Symbol   Name                        Unit    Valid Range
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  C        Fruit Color / Maturity      —       Green / Yellow / Brown
  L        Fruit Length                cm      3.0 – 8.0 cm
  W        Fruit Width                 cm      1.5 – 6.0 cm
  K        Estimated Kernel Mass       g       0.1 – 0.9 g
  WF       Estimated Whole Fruit Wt.   g       15.0 – 60.0 g

  NOTE: L, W, K, and WF are all estimated from the photo using the
  five-peso (P5) coin as the real-world size reference. These values
  directly push the final oil yield up or down from the color baseline.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 THE FORMULA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  y_hat = Y_base + dK + dL + dA + dW

  Then always clipped to the scientifically valid range:

  y_final = clamp(y_hat, 45%, 65%)


  Where each term is:

  Term     Name                  How it's computed                        Max effect
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Y_base   Color baseline yield  Looked up from color:                    Dominant
                                   Green  = 47.0%
                                   Yellow = 58.5%
                                   Brown  = 55.5%

  dK       Kernel mass adj.      From K relative to range 0.1–0.9 g      ±2%
             kernel_norm = (K - 0.1) / (0.9 - 0.1)       → 0.0 to 1.0
             dK          = (kernel_norm - 0.5) × 4.0      → -2% to +2%

  dL       Length adjustment     clamp(L - 5.0, -2.0, +3.0)              -2% to +3%
             Longer than 5 cm average → positive
             Shorter than 5 cm → negative

  dA       Aspect ratio adj.     clamp((L/W - 1.43) × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0)  ±2%
             L/W = elongation ratio of the fruit
             1.43 = reference ratio (5.0 cm / 3.5 cm = average Talisay)
             More elongated than 1.43 → positive

  dW       Whole-fruit wt. adj.  clamp((WF - 35.0) / 20.0 × 1.5,        ±1.5%
                                         -1.5, +2.0)
             Heavier than 35 g average → positive
             Lighter than 35 g → negative

  Reference values (the "average" Talisay fruit):
    L = 5.0 cm,  W = 3.5 cm,  aspect = 1.43,  WF = 35 g
  A fruit bigger/heavier than these pushes yield UP.
  A fruit smaller/lighter pushes yield DOWN.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 EXAMPLE A — GREEN FRUIT (Immature)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Sample fruit:  L = 4.5 cm,  W = 3.0 cm,  K = 0.35 g,  WF = 28 g

  Step         Calculation                                        Value
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Y_base       Green baseline                                     47.00%

  dK           kernel_norm = (0.35 - 0.1) / 0.8 = 0.313
               dK = (0.313 - 0.5) × 4.0 = -0.750                -0.75%

  dL           clamp(4.5 - 5.0, -2.0, +3.0) = -0.500            -0.50%

  dA           4.5 / 3.0 = 1.500
               clamp((1.500 - 1.43) × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = +0.105 +0.11%

  dW           clamp((28 - 35) / 20 × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = -0.525 -0.53%
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Sum:         47.00 - 0.75 - 0.50 + 0.11 - 0.53              = 45.33%
  Clamp:       45.33% is within [45%, 65%]

  RESULT:  45.33%   |   Category: Below Average

  Interpretation: Immature fruit with a small kernel and low weight.
  Nearly hits the biological minimum floor of 45%.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 CONFIDENCE LEVEL — HOW IT IS COMPUTED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The Confidence IS NOT derived from the oil yield percentage itself.
  It measures how much the two ML models (Random Forest and Gradient
  Boosting) AGREE with each other after independently predicting the
  yield from the same 7 input features.

  Formula:
    Confidence = max(0.50,  1.0 - (|RF_pred - GB_pred| / 10))

  Variables:
    RF_pred   = Oil yield predicted by the Random Forest model (%)
    GB_pred   = Oil yield predicted by the Gradient Boosting model (%)
    |diff|    = Absolute disagreement between the two models
    0.50      = Minimum confidence floor — never drops below 50%
    /10       = Scaling factor: a 10% disagreement = 0% confidence above floor

  Worked examples:

    Scan      RF_pred   GB_pred   |diff|   Confidence
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Green     45.10%    45.55%    0.45     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.045) = 95.5%
    Yellow    59.50%    59.90%    0.40     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.040) = 96.0%
    Brown     58.10%    58.65%    0.55     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.055) = 94.5%
    Low agree 53.00%    58.00%    5.00     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.500) = 50.0%

  Interpretation:
    Models agree within ~1%  → confidence ~90–99%  → reliable result
    Models disagree by 5%    → confidence = 50.0%  → ambiguous fruit
    The final displayed oil yield = (RF_pred + GB_pred) / 2

 HOW THE MODEL ESTIMATES KERNEL MASS (K) AND FRUIT WEIGHT (WF)
================================================================================

  K (Kernel Mass) and WF (Whole Fruit Weight) are NOT directly measured.
  They are mathematically derived from L and W, which are measured from
  the photo using the Five-Peso (P5) coin as a real-world size reference.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 STEP 0 — MEASURING L AND W FROM THE PHOTO
 (₱5 Coin Reference)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The Philippine Five-Peso coin has a fixed known diameter:

        ₱5 coin diameter  =  25 mm  =  2.50 cm

  When the coin is visible in the photo, the system detects it and
  computes a pixel-to-centimeter conversion:

        pixels_per_cm  =  coin_diameter_in_pixels / 2.50

  This scale factor is then applied to the fruit's pixel dimensions:

        L (cm)  =  fruit_length_in_pixels  /  pixels_per_cm
        W (cm)  =  fruit_width_in_pixels   /  pixels_per_cm

  Both are clamped to valid ranges:
        L  →  [3.0, 8.0] cm
        W  →  [1.5, 6.0] cm

  If no coin is detected, the system estimates L and W from the
  fruit's bounding box aspect ratio using Talisay priors (L ≈ 5.0 cm,
  W ≈ 3.5 cm as baseline), but accuracy is lower.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 KERNEL MASS ESTIMATION — Formula
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Source: ml/predict.py  →  _estimate_kernel_mass(L, W)

        size_factor  =  (L × W) / (5.0 × 3.5)

        K  =  0.4 × size_factor

        K_final  =  clamp(K,  0.1,  0.9)  g

  Explanation:
    - The reference fruit (L=5.0 cm, W=3.5 cm) has a kernel mass of
      exactly 0.4 g — the empirical median for Talisay kernels.
    - Any fruit bigger or smaller scales that baseline proportionally.
    - Result is always kept within the validated range [0.1 g, 0.9 g].

  Worked Example — Yellow fruit: L = 5.5 cm, W = 4.0 cm

    Step         Calculation                                    Value
    ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    size_factor  (5.5 × 4.0) / (5.0 × 3.5)  =  22.0 / 17.5  = 1.257
    K            0.4 × 1.257                                  = 0.503 g
    Clamp        0.503 is within [0.1, 0.9]  → no clamp
    K_final                                                    = 0.503 g


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 WHOLE FRUIT WEIGHT ESTIMATION — Formula
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Source: ml/predict.py  →  _estimate_fruit_weight(L, W)

  The fruit is modeled as a prolate ellipsoid with three semi-axes:

        a  =  L / 2               (half-length)
        b  =  W / 2               (half-width)
        c  =  (L + W) / 4         (average radius)

  Ellipsoid volume formula:

        V  =  (4/3) × π × a × b × c

  Weight estimate (density factor = 0.8 g/cm³):

        WF  =  V × 0.8

        WF_final  =  clamp(WF,  15.0,  60.0)  g

  Explanation:
    - The 0.8 g/cm³ density factor reflects Talisay fruit's average
      fresh density (slightly less than water due to fibrous husk).
    - Result is always kept within the validated range [15.0 g, 60.0 g].

  Worked Example — Yellow fruit: L = 5.5 cm, W = 4.0 cm

    Step         Calculation                                         Value
    ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    a            5.5 / 2                                           = 2.75 cm
    b            4.0 / 2                                           = 2.00 cm
    c            (5.5 + 4.0) / 4                                   = 2.375 cm
    V            (4/3) × π × 2.75 × 2.00 × 2.375                  = 54.64 cm³
    WF           54.64 × 0.8                                        = 43.71 g
    Clamp        43.71 is within [15.0, 60.0] → no clamp
    WF_final                                                        = 43.71 g

 ACTUAL OIL EXTRACTION — MEASURING AND VERIFYING OIL YIELD
================================================================================

  The oil yield percentage used throughout this system is defined as:

        Oil Yield (%)  =  (mass of extracted oil in g / mass of dry kernel in g)  × 100

  This is the same basis used by all cited research studies (Soxhlet
  extraction, dry-weight kernel basis).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 LAB PROCEDURE — Soxhlet Extraction (Standard Method)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Step   Action
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1      Crack open the Talisay fruit and remove the inner kernel.
  2      Dry the kernel in an oven at 60°C for 24 hours (or until
         the weight does not change between weighings).
  3      Weigh the oven-dried kernel on a precision balance.
         Record this as  m_kernel (g).
  4      Load the dry kernel into a Soxhlet extractor.
         Use petroleum ether or n-hexane as the extraction solvent.
  5      Run the extraction for 6–8 hours at the solvent's boiling point.
  6      Evaporate the solvent using a rotary evaporator or a water
         bath set to 60°C. Allow residual solvent to fully evaporate.
  7      Weigh the recovered oil in a pre-weighed container.
         Record this as  m_oil (g).
  8      Calculate the actual oil yield percentage.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 OIL YIELD CALCULATION — From Lab Results
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

        Oil Yield_actual (%)  =  (m_oil / m_kernel) × 100

  Example:
    m_kernel  =  0.52 g   (weighed dry kernel)
    m_oil     =  0.305 g  (weighed extracted oil)

    Oil Yield_actual  =  (0.305 / 0.52) × 100  =  58.65%


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 VERIFICATION — Comparing System vs. Actual Lab Result
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Use the same fruit that was scanned by the system. After scanning:
    1. Extract the kernel and perform the Soxhlet procedure above.
    2. Compare the two percentages side by side.

  Full example using the Yellow fruit (Example B):

                           System Prediction     Lab Result
    ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Kernel Mass (K)         0.503 g (estimated)  0.52 g (actual dry wt)
    Predicted Oil Mass      0.503 × 0.597 ≈ 0.300 g
    Extracted Oil (m_oil)   —                    0.305 g
    Oil Yield (%)           59.70%               58.65%
    Difference              —                    1.05%  ✓ Match
    ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Verdict                 EXCELLENT MATCH  (difference < 2%)


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 MATCH CRITERIA — Acceptable Tolerance
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Difference (System vs. Lab)    Interpretation
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  < 2.0%                         Excellent match
  2.0% – 5.0%                    Good match (normal lab variation)
  > 5.0%                         Review needed — see notes below


  Common causes of > 5% discrepancy:
    • Kernel was not fully dry before weighing (moisture adds false mass)
    • Solvent was not fully evaporated before weighing the oil
    • ₱5 coin was absent or partially obscured in the scanned photo
      (system fell back to aspect-ratio estimation for L and W)
    • Fruit was damaged, cracked, or had mold affecting kernel density

  IMPORTANT: Always use oven-dried kernels (60°C, 24 hours) to match
  the dry-weight basis assumed by the cited literature and this system.
