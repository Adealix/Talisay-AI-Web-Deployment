   TALISAY AI — OIL YIELD CALCULATION: SIMPLIFIED SOLUTION GUIDE
   Single Analysis Scan — Green, Yellow, and Brown Fruits
================================================================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 VARIABLES (Inputs to the Formula)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Symbol   Name                        Unit    Valid Range
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  C        Fruit Color / Maturity      —       Green / Yellow / Brown
  L        Fruit Length                cm      3.0 – 8.0 cm
  W        Fruit Width                 cm      1.5 – 6.0 cm
  K        Estimated Kernel Mass       g       0.1 – 0.9 g
  WF       Estimated Whole Fruit Wt.   g       15.0 – 60.0 g

  NOTE: L, W, K, and WF are all estimated from the photo using the
  five-peso (P5) coin as the real-world size reference. These values
  directly push the final oil yield up or down from the color baseline.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 THE FORMULA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  y_hat = Y_base + dK + dL + dA + dW

  Then always clipped to the scientifically valid range:

  y_final = clamp(y_hat, 45%, 65%)


  Where each term is:

  Term     Name                  How it's computed                        Max effect
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Y_base   Color baseline yield  Looked up from color:                    Dominant
                                   Green  = 47.0%
                                   Yellow = 58.5%
                                   Brown  = 55.5%

  dK       Kernel mass adj.      From K relative to range 0.1–0.9 g      ±2%
             kernel_norm = (K - 0.1) / (0.9 - 0.1)       → 0.0 to 1.0
             dK          = (kernel_norm - 0.5) × 4.0      → -2% to +2%

  dL       Length adjustment     clamp(L - 5.0, -2.0, +3.0)              -2% to +3%
             Longer than 5 cm average → positive
             Shorter than 5 cm → negative

  dA       Aspect ratio adj.     clamp((L/W - 1.43) × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0)  ±2%
             L/W = elongation ratio of the fruit
             1.43 = reference ratio (5.0 cm / 3.5 cm = average Talisay)
             More elongated than 1.43 → positive

  dW       Whole-fruit wt. adj.  clamp((WF - 35.0) / 20.0 × 1.5,        ±1.5%
                                         -1.5, +2.0)
             Heavier than 35 g average → positive
             Lighter than 35 g → negative

  Reference values (the "average" Talisay fruit):
    L = 5.0 cm,  W = 3.5 cm,  aspect = 1.43,  WF = 35 g
  A fruit bigger/heavier than these pushes yield UP.
  A fruit smaller/lighter pushes yield DOWN.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 EXAMPLE A — GREEN FRUIT (Immature)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Sample fruit:  L = 4.5 cm,  W = 3.0 cm,  K = 0.35 g,  WF = 28 g

  Step         Calculation                                        Value
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Y_base       Green baseline                                     47.00%

  dK           kernel_norm = (0.35 - 0.1) / 0.8 = 0.313
               dK = (0.313 - 0.5) × 4.0 = -0.750                -0.75%

  dL           clamp(4.5 - 5.0, -2.0, +3.0) = -0.500            -0.50%

  dA           4.5 / 3.0 = 1.500
               clamp((1.500 - 1.43) × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = +0.105 +0.11%

  dW           clamp((28 - 35) / 20 × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = -0.525 -0.53%
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Sum:         47.00 - 0.75 - 0.50 + 0.11 - 0.53              = 45.33%
  Clamp:       45.33% is within [45%, 65%]

  RESULT:  45.33%   |   Category: Below Average

  Interpretation: Immature fruit with a small kernel and low weight.
  Nearly hits the biological minimum floor of 45%.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 EXAMPLE B — YELLOW FRUIT (Mature / Peak Yield)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Sample fruit:  L = 5.5 cm,  W = 4.0 cm,  K = 0.55 g,  WF = 42 g

  Step         Calculation                                        Value
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Y_base       Yellow baseline                                    58.50%

  dK           kernel_norm = (0.55 - 0.1) / 0.8 = 0.563
               dK = (0.563 - 0.5) × 4.0 = +0.250                +0.25%

  dL           clamp(5.5 - 5.0, -2.0, +3.0) = +0.500            +0.50%

  dA           5.5 / 4.0 = 1.375
               clamp((1.375 - 1.43) × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = -0.082 -0.08%

  dW           clamp((42 - 35) / 20 × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = +0.525 +0.53%
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Sum:         58.50 + 0.25 + 0.50 - 0.08 + 0.53              = 59.70%
  Clamp:       59.70% is within [45%, 65%]

  RESULT:  59.69%   |   Category: Excellent

  Interpretation: A well-developed mature fruit with good dimensions.
  Comfortably in the "Excellent" tier (>=58%).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 EXAMPLE C — BROWN FRUIT (Fully Ripe)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Sample fruit:  L = 6.0 cm,  W = 4.2 cm,  K = 0.65 g,  WF = 50 g

  Step         Calculation                                        Value
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Y_base       Brown baseline                                     55.50%

  dK           kernel_norm = (0.65 - 0.1) / 0.8 = 0.688
               dK = (0.688 - 0.5) × 4.0 = +0.750                +0.75%

  dL           clamp(6.0 - 5.0, -2.0, +3.0) = +1.000            +1.00%

  dA           6.0 / 4.2 = 1.429
               clamp((1.429 - 1.43) × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) ≈ 0.000  ~0.00%

  dW           clamp((50 - 35) / 20 × 1.5, -1.5, +2.0) = +1.125 +1.13%
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Sum:         55.50 + 0.75 + 1.00 + 0.00 + 1.13              = 58.38%
  Clamp:       58.38% is within [45%, 65%]

  RESULT:  58.37%   |   Category: Excellent

  Interpretation: A large, heavy, fully ripe fruit. Despite the lower
  Brown baseline (55.5%), its big size drives it into "Excellent" territory.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 CONFIDENCE LEVEL — HOW IT IS COMPUTED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The Confidence IS NOT derived from the oil yield percentage itself.
  It measures how much the two ML models (Random Forest and Gradient
  Boosting) AGREE with each other after independently predicting the
  yield from the same 7 input features.

  Formula:
    Confidence = max(0.50,  1.0 - (|RF_pred - GB_pred| / 10))

  Variables:
    RF_pred   = Oil yield predicted by the Random Forest model (%)
    GB_pred   = Oil yield predicted by the Gradient Boosting model (%)
    |diff|    = Absolute disagreement between the two models
    0.50      = Minimum confidence floor — never drops below 50%
    /10       = Scaling factor: a 10% disagreement = 0% confidence above floor

  Worked examples:

    Scan      RF_pred   GB_pred   |diff|   Confidence
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Green     45.10%    45.55%    0.45     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.045) = 95.5%
    Yellow    59.50%    59.90%    0.40     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.040) = 96.0%
    Brown     58.10%    58.65%    0.55     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.055) = 94.5%
    Low agree 53.00%    58.00%    5.00     max(0.50, 1.0 - 0.500) = 50.0%

  Interpretation:
    Models agree within ~1%  → confidence ~90–99%  → reliable result
    Models disagree by 5%    → confidence = 50.0%  → ambiguous fruit
    The final displayed oil yield = (RF_pred + GB_pred) / 2
